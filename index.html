What Is Cosmos ?

ما الكون ؟

Cosmos refers to the universe in its entirety, encompassing all matter, energy, space, and time. It includes galaxies, stars, planets, and everything in between, extending infinitely beyond what we can observe. The study of cosmos, known as cosmology, explores the origins, evolution, and ultimate fate of the universe. It delves into fundamental questions about the nature of reality, the existence of dark matter and dark energy, and the possibility of other life forms beyond Earth. Cosmos captivates human curiosity, inspiring scientific inquiry, philosophical contemplation, and artistic expression as we strive to understand our place in the vast expanse of space.

Cosmic Statistics:

Observable Universe Size

The observable universe is approximately 93 billion light-years in diameter, containing an estimated 2 trillion galaxies, each with billions of stars.

The Hubble Deep Field

The Hubble Space Telescope's Deep Field image revealed over 3,000 galaxies in a patch of sky that appears the size of a grain of sand held at arm's length. This image shows galaxies as far as 13 billion light-years away.

Stars in the Milky Way

Our Milky Way galaxy contains an estimated 100 to 400 billion stars. If you could count one star per second, it would take you over 12,000 years to count them all.
